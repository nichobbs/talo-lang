# City win the cup in the last minute (a match report)

- **Domain:** sport — the corpus's first, and the largest untouched vocabulary area.
- **Source:** original football match report, written then translated.
- **Gaps found & filled (a real batch — sport was under-covered):** `timu` team
  (SOC-131), `sinda` win (ACT-156; **lose = `sindaku`** via the opposite affix),
  `goluka` goal/point (SOC-132), `ahili` last/final/end (TIM-051). Reused: player =
  `asobukika` (play+agent), captain = `pipikika` (lead+agent), coach = `ayalikika`
  (train+agent), cup/trophy = `hosuka` (prize), draw = `sawape` (equal), beat =
  `sinda`, crowd = `gunu`, fan = `kipa`, celebrate = `iwau`. Team names generalized
  to "the city team / the other team"; "give up" rendered as `cobato hata ahili`
  ("tried to the end").

## English

The city team won the cup last night, beating the other team two goals to one in a
big final.

The other team scored first, in the tenth minute. But the city team played better in
the second half. Their captain scored after an hour, and the two teams were equal.

Then, in the last minute, a young player kicked the ball into the net, and the crowd
shouted with joy. The city team had won.

"Our team played with heart," said the coach. "We tried right to the end." The
players lifted the cup, and thousands of fans celebrated in the streets.
