# Prices rise as families struggle (an economy report)

- **Domain:** finance / economics — cost of living, debt, wages.
- **Source:** original economy news item, written then translated.
- **Gaps found:** none — the finance lexicon was already deep (`deuda` debt, `debe`
  owe, `kalile` borrow, `kasua` lend, `kasegu` earn, `gasa` spend, `kulo` wage,
  `kodi` tax, `libun` profit, `masini`/`umasini` poor/poverty, `tenaga` energy,
  `kawati` worry, `seifu` government). "Loan" = `kalileboka` (borrow+result),
  "save (money)" = `gudan uanka` (keep money), "interest rate" paraphrased as the
  cost of borrowing, "economist" = `hitoka daganka fe` (trade people).
- **Grammar exercised:** `naiki`/`anguka` rise/fall, `lebi`/`sukuna` more/less,
  causative `tenda … takaiape lebi`, reported speech, `lasima` "must".

## English

Prices are rising, and many families are struggling. Last month the cost of living
rose again, and many families find it hard to pay. Food and energy prices rose the
most.

The bank made borrowing more expensive, to slow the rising prices. But people with
debts now pay more each month.

"We earn the same, but everything costs more," said one person. "We save less money,
and we worry about the future."

Economists say wages must rise too, or many families will fall into poverty. The
government has promised help for the very poor, but some say it is not enough.
