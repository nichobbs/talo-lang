# A letter to a friend

- **Register:** personal correspondence — informal, first-person, affectionate.
- **Source:** original, written as a real letter first, then translated.
- **What it stress-tests (vs. third-person news):** sustained **1st/2nd person**;
  **future plans** (`keso` + `tumaini`); **emotion** (`senan`, `suki`, the new
  `lindu` "miss"); **direct questions to the reader** (`Yu omouto seko?`,
  `… ke?`); a letter's **greeting and sign-off**.
- **Gaps found & handled:** the lexicon was again nearly complete — `tegami` letter,
  `belita` news, `masulo` busy, `gela` move, `kowen` park, `temu` meet/visit,
  `lencana` plan, `afiya` health, `ciu` kiss, `salin` together/each-other all
  existed; seasons paraphrase as `kisesu panape` (hot season = summer). The one
  genuine gap — the emotion **"to miss / long for"** — is filled this batch as
  `lindu`. Names generalized to "dear friend / your friend" (corpus convention).

## English

Dear friend,

Thank you for your letter. I was very happy when I read it. Sorry I did not write
sooner — I have been very busy.

Last month I moved to a new city, for work. The house is small but it is nice, and
there is a park nearby. I miss my old friends, but I am making new ones.

Next summer I hope to come and visit you. Maybe we can travel together. What do you
think? Are you still living in the same place?

Please write soon, and tell me your news. I hope you and your family are well.

With love, your friend.

## Talo

```talo
# --- greeting ---
Tomoka sukipe,                              › Dear friend,
# gloss: friend-N beloved-MOD

# --- thanks and apology (sa "when"; masulo "busy") ---
Tanku fo tegamika yu.                       › Thank you for your letter.
# gloss: thanks for letter-N your
Mi yato senanpe sana, sa mi bacato te.      › I was very happy when I read it.
# gloss: I COP happy-MOD very, when I read-V it
Pole, mi ne tulito hayaipe.                  › Sorry I did not write sooner.
# gloss: sorry, I NEG write-V early
Mi yato masulope sana.                      › I have been very busy.
# gloss: I COP busy-MOD very

# --- the news (gela "move"; lindu "miss", new) ---
Naka lunka mae, mi gelato kotaka nayape su, fo kasika. › Last month I moved to a new city, for work.
# gloss: in month-N before, I move-V city-N new-MOD toward, for work-N
Baitika yato pikope, ma te yato haope, i kowenka kunato cikaipe. › The house is small, but it is nice, and there is a park nearby.
# gloss: house-N COP small-MOD, but it COP good-MOD, and park-N exist near-MOD
Mi linduto tomoka tuape mi pu, ma mi tendato tomoka nayape. › I miss my old friends, but I am making new ones.
# gloss: I miss-V friend-N old-MOD my PL, but I make-V friend-N new-MOD

# --- plans and questions (future via keso + tumaini; direct questions) ---
Naka kisesuka panape keso, mi tumainito ce mi datanto i temuto yu. › Next summer I hope to come and visit you.
# gloss: in season-N hot-MOD next, I hope-V that I come-V and meet-V you
Mungi mi pu bekito tabito salin.            › Maybe we can travel together.
# gloss: maybe we PL can travel-V together
Yu omouto seko?                             › What do you think?
# gloss: you think-V what
Yu masi hiduto naka lokaka samape ke?       › Are you still living in the same place?
# gloss: you still live-V in place-N same-MOD Q

# --- sign-off (imperative request; afiya "health") ---
Doso, yu tulito keso, i yu semato belitaka yu mi na. › Please write soon, and tell me your news.
# gloss: please, you write-V soon, and you tell-V news-N your me to
Mi tumainito ce yu i familika yu yato afiyape. › I hope you and your family are well.
# gloss: I hope-V that you and family-N your COP healthy-MOD
Sukika wa, tomoka yu.                        › With love, your friend.
# gloss: love-N with, friend-N your
```
