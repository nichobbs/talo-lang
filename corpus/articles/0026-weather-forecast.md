# Five-day weather forecast (a data-dense bulletin)

- **Register:** data / bulletin — terse, number-dense, fragment-heavy.
- **Source:** original, written as a normal forecast and then translated.
- **What it stress-tests (the newest, least-exercised machinery, all at once):**
  **weekday compounds** (`nudinka` Mon … `codinka` Fri — 0016 dates); **temperatures**
  (`dalayaka` + numeral); **numeric ranges** (`dalayaka diko su diko le` "10–15
  degrees" — 0018); **percentages** (`pasento ki diko` 70 %); **times of day**
  (`asagohan`/`petana`/`usiku`); verbless headline fragments; one imperative.
- **Gaps found:** none — the weather lexicon was already complete (`mausa`, `taio`,
  `awan` cloud, `ame` rain, `yuki` snow, `angin` wind, `tufani` storm, `petana`
  afternoon, `sumatu` weekend). "Sunny" = `taiope`, "chance of rain" = `mungi … +
  pasento`, "temperature" = `dalayaka`. Multi-digit numbers are written as separate
  atomic tokens (`nu diko nu` = 22) so each glosses.

## English

**Five-day weather forecast**

Today, Monday: Sunny and warm. 22 degrees by day, 14 at night. A light wind from the west.

Tuesday: Cloudy in the morning, then rain in the afternoon. Ten to fifteen degrees. A seventy per cent chance of rain.

Wednesday: Heavy rain and a strong wind — only nine degrees. Stay at home.

Thursday: The rain will clear. Cold at night, about three degrees.

Friday: Sunny again, but cold — five to twelve degrees. The weekend will be warmer.

## Talo

```talo
# --- the bulletin (weekday compounds; dalayaka + numeral; ranges; pasento) ---
Mausaka fo dinka le:                         › Five-day weather forecast:
# gloss: weather-N for day-N 5

Leo, nudinka: taiope i atatakape.            › Today, Monday: sunny and warm.
# gloss: today, Monday: sunny-MOD and warm-MOD
Naka leo, dalayaka nu diko nu; naka usikuka, dalayaka diko hu. › 22 degrees by day; 14 at night.
# gloss: in day, degree-N 22; in night-N, degree-N 14
Anginka pikope, magalibika fe.               › A light wind, from the west.
# gloss: wind-N small-MOD, west-N from

Modinka: awanpe naka asagohanka, toki ameka naka petanaka. › Tuesday: cloudy in the morning, then rain in the afternoon.
# gloss: Tuesday: cloudy-MOD in morning-N, then rain-N in afternoon-N
Dalayaka diko su diko le.                    › Ten to fifteen degrees.
# gloss: degree-N 10 to 15
Mungi ameka, pasento ki diko.                › A seventy per cent chance of rain.
# gloss: maybe rain-N, percent 70

Hudinka: ameka gandepe i anginka tuyoipe, dalayaka po dake. › Wednesday: heavy rain and a strong wind — only nine degrees.
# gloss: Wednesday: rain-N big-MOD and wind-N strong-MOD, degree-N 9 only
Yu tomaluto naka baitika.                    › Stay at home.
# gloss: you stay-V in house-N

Ledinka: ameka wasito. Holope naka usikuka, dalayaka mo. › Thursday: the rain will clear. Cold at night, about three degrees.
# gloss: Thursday: rain-N clear-V. cold-MOD in night-N, degree-N 3

Codinka: taiope lagia, ma holope. Dalayaka le su diko nu. › Friday: sunny again, but cold. Five to twelve degrees.
# gloss: Friday: sunny-MOD again, but cold-MOD. degree-N 5 to 12
Sumatuka yato atatakape lebi.                › The weekend will be warmer.
# gloss: weekend-N COP warm-MOD more
```
