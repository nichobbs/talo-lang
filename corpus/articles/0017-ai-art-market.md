# AI-generated art reshapes the market

- **Register:** feature / news analysis (arts + technology).
- **Source:** agent-generated, written in a BBC-style for this corpus — not

- **Proper nouns:** generalized in the Talo (a gallery curator, an art historian,

- **Grammar exercised (0020):** caritive `tanpa` "without"; embedded yes/no with

## English

The art world is facing a crisis as artificial intelligence tools that create
paintings and sculptures in seconds flood the market with cheap alternatives,
museum curators and artists warn.

Auction houses have begun refusing to handle works made entirely by AI, while
galleries cannot tell human-made and machine-made pieces apart. The uncertainty is
already changing prices for new artists and threatening long-established careers.

A gallery curator said: "We are seeing a fundamental shift in how art is valued. If
a good picture can be made for £50, why would a collector buy an unknown artist's
work for £5,000?"

The technology is now far more powerful, and many people can use it. Artists can
make good work without training, and companies make many pictures every day. Demand
for art as paid work has dropped.

Institutions now ask what authentic art really is. A panel discussed whether AI art
may enter museums; no agreement emerged. Some say AI is only a new tool, as
photography was before; others say art needs a human mind.

"A photograph needed a photographer, even though a machine made the picture," said
an art historian. "But who is the true maker of an AI picture? The answer is
genuinely unclear."

The law is also unclear. In America the law says such art has no rights, because it
is made without a human. Artists are fighting the companies in court, because the
firms trained their systems on millions of pictures without permission.

Some artists are fighting back: they say their work is authentic, and sell it at a
high price. Others use AI as a tool, as people once used photography. For now the
big museums stay with human artists; but as the machine grows, they may not be able
to hold that position.
