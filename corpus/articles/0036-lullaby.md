# A lullaby (a song)

- **Register:** song / verse — a lullaby, with a repeated refrain, regular short
  lines, and end-rhyme. The corpus's first piece written to be *sung*.
- **Source:** composed natively in Talo (so it can actually scan and rhyme), with
  a singable English rendering alongside — not a translation of an English song.
- **What it tests — does Talo rhyme?** Yes, and a finding falls out: because every
  content word ends in its POS badge (`-ka` N · `-to` V · `-pe` MOD), Talo rhymes
  **by grammatical category** — any two nouns share an `-a` ending, etc. That makes
  easy assonance; **true rhyme** comes when the syllable *before* the badge matches
  too — e.g. `totoka` / `moyoka` (child / heart), `sisukape` / `osoipe`. Function
  words (`yu`, `mi`, `su`) give other line-endings when wanted.
- **Gaps found:** none — `utau` sing, `wimo` song, `yume` dream, `bulan` moon,
  `hosi` star, `moyo` heart, `nagale` flow all existed.

## English

*(refrain)*
Sleep now, little one;
I stay near you, and hold you in my heart.

The moon climbs up the sky;
the little stars are singing you a song.

The wind is speaking softly;
the river, flowing slowly, slips away.

*(refrain)*
Sleep now, little one;
I stay near you, and hold you in my heart.

## Talo

```talo
# --- refrain (R) — true rhyme: totoka / moyoka (child / heart) ---
Yu tiduto inino, totoka. › Sleep now, little one;
# gloss: you sleep-V now, child-N
Mi tomaluto yu wa, i mi peganto moyoka. › I stay near you, and hold [you in my] heart.
# gloss: I stay-V you with, and I hold-V heart-N

# --- verse 1 (utau "sing"; wimo "song") ---
Bulanka naikito naka solaka. › The moon climbs up the sky;
# gloss: moon-N rise-V in sky-N
Hosika pu utauto wimoka. › the little stars are singing you a song.
# gloss: star-N PL sing-V song-N

# --- verse 2 — rhyme: sisukape / osoipe (softly / slowly) ---
Anginka semato sisukape. › The wind is speaking softly;
# gloss: wind-N speak-V calm-MOD
Kawaka nagaleto osoipe. › the river, flowing slowly, slips away.
# gloss: river-N flow-V slow-MOD

# --- refrain (R) ---
Yu tiduto inino, totoka. › Sleep now, little one;
# gloss: you sleep-V now, child-N
Mi tomaluto yu wa, i mi peganto moyoka. › I stay near you, and hold [you in my] heart.
# gloss: I stay-V you with, and I hold-V heart-N
```
