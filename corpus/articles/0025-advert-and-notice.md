# A market advert and a park notice (persuasive register)

- **Register:** persuasive / commercial + regulatory — a shop advert and a public
  notice of rules. Direct 2nd-person address, exhortation, prohibition.
- **Source:** original, written as a normal advert and notice, then translated.
- **What it stress-tests (the last untested grammar):** **deontic modality** —
  permission `bekito` "may", obligation `lasimato` "must", **prohibition** via
  `kena katasato` "is forbidden"; **negative imperatives** `ne yu …` "do not …";
  **superlatives** `lebi ote fe` "the best"; exclamations and 2nd-person commands.
- **Gaps found:** **none** — the deontic + commercial lexicon was already complete
  (`isini` allow, `lasima` must, `katasa` forbid, `mise` shop, `buka`/`fungai`
  open/close, `usiku` night, `sawadi` gift). "Free of charge" renders as
  `nedanka tanpa` (without a price), "sale" as `yualaka gandepe` (a big selling),
  "best" as the superlative. This register needed no new roots — only grammar the
  earlier registers never reached.

## English

### Big summer sale!

Come to the market this week! All our fruit is fresh, and our prices are the best in
the city. Buy three oranges, and get one free! Do not miss it. We are open every day,
from morning until night.

### Park rules

- You may walk on the grass.
- Please do not pick the flowers.
- Dogs must stay on the path.
- Fires are forbidden.
- Please keep the park clean.
