# Opinion: study matters

- **Register:** opinion / analysis (the hardest register so far — abstract,
  argued, evaluative)
- **Source:** original op-ed-style piece (not a reproduction)
- **Proper nouns:** none
- **Purpose:** deliberately probe where the grammar **strains**. The piece is
  fully valid Talo, but several natural opinion constructions had to be
  paraphrased — see `corpus/GAPS.md` "Register strains".
- **Uses the new roots:** `soma` study, `bahaya` danger, `tomalu` stay/remain.

## English

Study is very important for every country. I think children must study. Those who
study know more. Study matters more than money. If a country helps its schools,
then it grows strong. But some leaders do not see the danger. They think money is
the first thing. This is a big mistake. We must study, because knowledge stays. A
good country studies and stays strong.

## Talo

```talo
# 1 — abstract subject (study-as-noun); muhimu "important"; kila "every"; na "for"
somaka yato muhimupe sana negalaka kila na     › Study is very important for every country.
# gloss: study-N COP important-MOD very country-N every for

# 2–3 — embedded thought by juxtaposition (no "that"; see GAPS); modality lasima
#        as verb + nominalised complement ("must the-studying")
mi omouto li                                    › I think:
# gloss: I think-V COMPLETIVE
totoka pu lasimato somaka                        › children must study.
# gloss: child-N PL must-V study-N

# 4 — relative "those who study" rendered as the agent noun somakika
somakika tauto lebi                              › Those who study know more.
# gloss: studier-N know-V more

# 5–6 — comparative "more than" has no standard marker: paraphrased as two
#        contrasting degree clauses (lebi "more" / sukuna "less")
somaka yato muhimupe lebi                         › Study matters more —
# gloss: study-N COP important-MOD more
uanka yato muhimupe sukuna                         › money matters less.
# gloss: money-N COP important-MOD less

# 7 — conditional with toki (0005 §1)
fi negalaka tolonato belayabaitika pu, toki te sodatuto tuyoipe   › If a country helps its schools, then it grows strong.
# gloss: if country-N help-V school-N PL, then it grow-V strong-MOD

# 8 — ma "but"; bahaya "danger" (new); negation ne
ma mukuka ne kanto bahayaka                       › But some leaders do not see the danger.
# gloss: but leader-N NEG see-V danger-N

# 9–10 — second embedded thought; ordinal tape... here mono tape "the first thing"
te omouto li                                      › They think:
# gloss: they think-V COMPLETIVE
uanka yato monoka tape                             › money is the first thing.
# gloss: money-N COP thing-N first

# 11 — evaluation
ini yato macigaika gandepe                         › This is a big mistake.
# gloss: this COP mistake-N big-MOD

# 12 — sababu "because"; tomalu "stay/remain" (new); wida "knowledge"
mi lasimato somaka sababu widaka tomaluto          › We must study, because knowledge stays.
# gloss: we must-V study-N because knowledge-N stay-V

# 13 — closing; i coordinates two predicates
negalaka haope somato i tomaluto tuyoipe           › A good country studies and stays strong.
# gloss: country-N good-MOD study-V and stay-V strong-MOD
```
