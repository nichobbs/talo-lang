# Israel and Lebanon agree on a ceasefire

- **Register:** hard news (international diplomacy / conflict)
- **Source:** original factual summary of a real news event (a US-announced
  Israel–Lebanon ceasefire, June 2026), written for this corpus — **not** a
  reproduction of any outlet's article text.
- **Proper nouns:** `Isalaeli` Israel, `Lebanon`, `Hesibola` Hezbollah, `Amelika`
  US/America, `Ilan` Iran, `Wasinton` Washington (`proper-nouns.tsv`).
- **New roots used (conflict batch):** `katasa` forbid, `hamala` attack, `buki`
  weapon — plus existing `setuyu` agree, `salama` safe, `guntai` army, `pelan`
  war, `amani` peace, `adui` enemy.
- "Ceasefire" itself was paraphrased ("agree to stop the war"); other gaps in
  `corpus/GAPS.md` ("Conflict/diplomacy article").

## English

Israel and Lebanon agreed. They will stop the war again. They will make safe
areas inside Lebanon. In these areas the two countries forbid Hezbollah's
fighters. America announced the agreement in Washington. If Hezbollah stops the
attacks, the agreement holds. Israel's army had killed some people in Lebanon, and
Hezbollah had sent weapons into Israel. Hezbollah is a very strong group; Iran
helps Hezbollah. Many countries say Hezbollah is an enemy. The two countries will
talk again. An Israeli minister said the agreement is a mistake; he said Hezbollah
will grow strong. The war has killed very many people. Many people left their
homes and now stay in far areas. Each side says the enemy is wrong. Now many
people want peace.
