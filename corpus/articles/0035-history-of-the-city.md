# A short history of the city

- **Domain:** history — time-depth narrative across centuries.
- **Source:** original short history, written then translated.
- **Gaps found:** none — the history/conflict lexicon was already deep (`talihi`
  history, `atita` past, `kiyiyi` village, `sekulo` century, `dindin` wall, `lango`
  gate, `adui` enemy, `guntai` army, `nasai` destroy, `yaku` burn, `kadimu`
  ancient, `batu` stone). Trader = `hitoka daganka fe` (trade people), "under the streets" =
  `naka cinika yalanka fe`, "around" = `kati`.
- **Grammar exercised:** deep past with `mae` "long ago/once", `selo` as a relative
  ("where the village once was"), `sodatu … naka` "grew into", a personifying close.

## English

Long ago, this place was only a small village, by the river. People grew food and
caught fish.

Many centuries ago, a king built a wall around the village, and it grew into a city.
Traders came from far countries, and the city became rich.

Then a great war came. Enemies destroyed the walls and burned the houses. For a
hundred years the place was empty.

Slowly, people returned. They built new houses on the old stones. Today a great city
stands where the village once was. But under the streets, the old walls still sleep.
