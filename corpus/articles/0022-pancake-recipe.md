# Simple pancakes (a recipe)

- **Register:** procedural / instructional — the first recipe in the corpus.
- **Source:** original, written as a normal recipe first, then translated.
- **What it stress-tests:** **imperatives in sequence** (2nd-person `yu` + verb,
  step by step); **measures** (`gamu` gram, `kikome` cup, `meni` minute);
  **`hata` "until"** (a result-endpoint clause); verbless ingredient fragments.
- **Gaps found & handled:** the cooking lexicon was nearly complete — `pika` cook,
  `golen` fry, `milana` mix, `kologa` stir, `yoda` add, `mimina` pour, `gali` heat,
  `kona` flour, `telu` egg, `susu` milk, `gula` sugar, `wan` bowl, `kiyiko` spoon
  all existed; `sufulia` already covers "pan", `kunin` (yellow) covers "golden",
  `pumika` "rest/remainder". The one genuine gap — the temporal subordinator
  **"until"** — is filled this batch as `hata`. "Pancake" is rendered as `lotika`
  (the bread/cake itself).

## English

**Simple pancakes** (makes eight)

Ingredients: 100 grams of flour, two eggs, two cups of milk, a little salt, a little oil.

1. Put the flour and the salt in a bowl.
2. Add the eggs and one cup of milk. Mix until the batter is smooth.
3. Add the rest of the milk, and stir it.
4. Heat a little oil in a pan.
5. Pour in a little batter. Cook for two minutes, until the bottom is golden.
6. Turn the pancake, and cook the other side for one minute.
7. Serve it hot, with sugar and lemon.
