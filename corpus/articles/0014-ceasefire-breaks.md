# When a ceasefire breaks

- **Register:** hard news (conflict / analysis)
- **Source:** original piece written for this corpus — a generic "ceasefire
  collapses" report, not about or reproduced from any specific outlet's article.
- **Proper nouns:** none (deliberately generic — "the two countries", "a militia").
- **New roots used (conflict batch 2):** `huduna` ceasefire/truce, `milisi`
  militia, `misile` rocket, `kisasi` revenge — plus existing `kosoa` negotiate,
  `inola` blame, `lela` tired, `tumaini` hope, `keli` real, `utali` north.

## English

The two countries agreed to a ceasefire. But the ceasefire did not hold. A militia
fired rockets into the north, and then the army attacked in revenge. The rockets
killed some people. Officials say the militia broke the ceasefire; but the militia
says the army started the fighting. Each country blames the enemy. The people want
a real ceasefire — they are tired of the war. Many families fled to safe areas.
Leaders will negotiate again next week, and they hope for peace. But the danger
remains big.
