# Numerals — times and fractions

- **Register:** grammar exercise (not news) — the smallest text that puts the
  `0010` numeral markers into running clauses.
- **Source:** original; constructed to exercise `0003` §5.1 / `0010` §2.2–2.3.
- **Proper nouns:** none
- **Exercises:** multiplicative `kai` (`NUM kai` = "N times"), fraction `bagi`
  (`a bagi b` = a ÷ b), alongside a bare cardinal. Numerals `ta`=1, `mo`=3.
  `kai`/`bagi` are closed-class markers (`0003` §5.1), so the gate accepts them
  without a lexicon row.

## English

I ran three times. She read the book once. I drank a third of the water.

## Talo

```talo
# 1 — multiplicative: NUM kai, post-verbal; hasilu "run" (ACT-005), mo = 3
mi hasiluto mo kai                  › I ran three times.
# gloss: I run-V three times

# 2 — multiplicative "once" = ta kai; baca "read" (SPE-007), hon "book" (MOD-002)
te bacato honka ta kai              › She read the book once.
# gloss: she read-V book-N one times

# 3 — fraction: NUM bagi NUM quantifying a noun; pina "drink" (BOD-029), pani "water" (PHY-008)
mi pinato panika ta bagi mo         › I drank a third of the water.
# gloss: I drink-V water-N one divide three
```
