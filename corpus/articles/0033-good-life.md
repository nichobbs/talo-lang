# What is a good life? (a reflection)

- **Domain:** philosophy / ethics — a reflective essay on meaning and the good life.
- **Source:** original reflection, written then translated.
- **Gaps found & filled:** one — `alati` "meaning / significance" (COG-094). The
  religious/ethical lexicon was otherwise deep (`tuhan` god, `loho` soul, `pecaya`
  believe, `agama` religion, `sula` heaven, `hekima` wise, `sacai` truth, `mukada`
  sacred, `oma` pray). "Virtue" → `haope`, "evil" → `baya`, "treat" → `tenda`,
  "others" → `hitoka hokape`, "way to live" → `padatika fo hiduka`.
- **Grammar exercised:** the **gnomic present** throughout; reported belief (`ce`);
  the manner equative `sewa` (the golden rule); `beki` "should"; superlative-free
  abstraction.

## English

People have always asked what makes a life good. Some say a good life is a life with
much money and power. Others say it is a life of pleasure. But many wise people, in
every age, have given a different answer.

They say: a good life is not what we have, but what we do. A good person helps
others, tells the truth, and is good even to strangers. Such a person may be poor,
but in the heart they are rich.

Some believe in a god, and some do not. But all agree on one thing: we should treat
others as we wish others to treat us. This simple rule is at the heart of every
religion.

In the end, perhaps the meaning of life is not a question to answer, but a way to
live.
