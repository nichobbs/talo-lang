# Court finds a man guilty of theft (a court report)

- **Domain:** law / justice — a court verdict.
- **Source:** original court report, written then translated.
- **Gaps found & filled:** one — `iba` "steal" (ACT-157 → theft `ibaboka`, thief
  `ibakika`). The legal lexicon was otherwise deep (`mahakama` court, `hukumu`
  judge, `gelesa` prison, `sumi` crime, `huku` punish, `adabu` punishment, `sahidu`
  witness, `yutisia` justice, `adili` fair, `laha` mercy, `yulusu` forgive).
  "Guilty" → paraphrased "the court found that he stole", lawyer = `hitoka seliaka
  fe`, employer = `pipikika` (boss).
- **Grammar exercised:** reported speech (`ce`), deontic `lasimato` "must", `pungusa`
  reduce, `su` (to/extent), comparatives.

## English

A court has found a man guilty of stealing money from his employer.

The man stole thousands of pounds over three years. The judge punished him: two
years in prison. He said he was sorry, but the judge said the law must be the same
for everyone.

His lawyer asked for a lighter punishment, because the man was poor and had a sick
child. The judge reduced the sentence to one year.

"Justice must be fair," said the judge. "But the law must also have mercy."
