# A storm is coming

- **Register:** hard news (weather warning)
- **Source:** original simplified item, BBC-style (not a verbatim reproduction)
- **Proper nouns:** none
- **Exercises (longer piece):** a **conditional** (`fi … , …`), a **wh-question**
  (`seko` in situ), **connectives** (`i` and, `ma` but, `sababu` because),
  **negative imperative** (`ne` + verb), progressive aspect `wi`, the goal marker
  `su`, and the time-word `keso` (tomorrow).

## English

A big storm is coming toward the city. The wind is very strong. The sea is high.
The government gave a warning. Officials said: if the rain comes, stay in the
house. Do not go to the sea. Close the doors, because the wind is strong. What
will you do tomorrow? Many people fear the storm, but the children are happy.
Tomorrow the sea will be good again.

## Talo

```talo
# 1 — progressive wi; goal su "toward"
tufanika gandepe datanto wi kotaka su          › A big storm is coming toward the city.
# gloss: storm-N big-MOD come-V PROGRESSIVE city-N toward

# 2–3 — property predicates on the copula
anginka yato tuyoipe sana                       › The wind is very strong.
# gloss: wind-N COP strong-MOD very
umika yato gandepe                              › The sea is high.
# gloss: sea-N COP big-MOD

# 4
seifuka donato keikokuka                         › The government gave a warning.
# gloss: government-N give-V warning-N

# 5 — reported speech + a conditional (fi …, result clause bare; 0005 §1)
adikalika semato li                              › Officials said:
# gloss: official-N say-V COMPLETIVE
fi ameka datanto, yu kunato baitika lo           › if the rain comes, stay in the house.
# gloss: if rain-N come-V, you be.at-V house-N at

# 6 — negative imperative: ne before the verb
yu ne ikuto umika su                             › Do not go to the sea.
# gloss: you NEG go-V sea-N toward

# 7 — sababu "because"
yu fungaito pintuka sababu anginka yato tuyoipe  › Close the doors, because the wind is strong.
# gloss: you close-V door-N because wind-N COP strong-MOD

# 8 — wh-question in situ: seko "what"
yu tendato seko keso                             › What will you do tomorrow?
# gloss: you do-V what tomorrow

# 9 — ma "but" joins the contrast
hitoka ingi takuto tufanika                      › Many people fear the storm,
# gloss: person-N many fear-V storm-N
ma totoka pu yato senanpe                        › but the children are happy.
# gloss: but child-N PL COP happy-MOD

# 10 — lagia "again"
keso umika yato haope lagia                      › Tomorrow the sea will be good again.
# gloss: tomorrow sea-N COP good-MOD again
```
