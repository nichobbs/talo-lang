# Opinion: peace is better than war

- **Register:** opinion / analysis — and the **regression piece for `docs/0012`**:
  it deliberately exercises every new construction (comparison `fe`, `-pe`
  relatives, serial modality + `beki`, complementiser `ce`).
- **Source:** original op-ed written for this corpus; not a reproduction.
- **Proper nouns:** none.
- **New this phase:** `beki` should, `ce` "that" (complementiser).

## English

Peace is better than war. I think that all countries should want peace. Leaders
who attack are wrong. A government should help the people who flee. Soldiers should
not kill children. The people know that war is very dangerous. A country that
negotiates is better than one that fights. I believe that peace can come. All
people should negotiate. Now the countries must agree to a ceasefire.

## Talo

```talo
# comparison: standard marked by fe "from" (ablative of comparison, 0012 §1)
amanika yato haope lebi pelanka fe                       › Peace is better than war.
# gloss: peace-N COP good-MOD more war-N than

# complementiser ce + serial modality (beki want): 0012 §3, §4
mi omouto ce negalaka ote bekito mauto amanika          › I think that all countries should want peace.
# gloss: I think-V that country-N all should-V want-V peace-N

# -pe participial relative, post-nominal (0012 §2)
mukuka hamalape yato salape                              › Leaders who attack are wrong.
# gloss: leader-N attacking-MOD COP wrong-MOD

# serial modal (beki) + main verb keeps its object; -pe relative (kimiape "fleeing")
seifuka bekito tolonato hitoka kimiape                   › A government should help the people who flee.
# gloss: government-N should-V help-V person-N fleeing-MOD

# negated modal: ne before the modal (0012 §3)
tentalaka ne bekito matitato totoka                      › Soldiers should not kill children.
# gloss: soldier-N NEG should-V kill-V child-N

# complementiser ce embedding a state predicate
hitoka tauto ce pelanka yato bahayape sana               › The people know that war is very dangerous.
# gloss: person-N know-V that war-N COP dangerous-MOD very

# comparison + two -pe relatives ("a country that negotiates" vs "one that fights")
negalaka kosoape yato haope lebi negalaka lagape fe       › A country that negotiates is better than one that fights.
# gloss: country-N negotiating-MOD COP good-MOD more country-N fighting-MOD than

# complementiser + serial modal (bisa "can")
mi pecayato ce amanika bisato datanto                    › I believe that peace can come.
# gloss: I believe-V that peace-N can-V come-V

ote hitoka bekito kosoato                                › All people should negotiate.
# gloss: all person-N should-V negotiate-V

inino negalaka lasimato setuyuto hudunaka                › Now the countries must agree to a ceasefire.
# gloss: now country-N must-V agree-V ceasefire-N
```
