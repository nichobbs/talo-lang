# A flood and a rescue

- **Register:** hard news (disaster)
- **Source:** original simplified item, BBC-style (not a verbatim reproduction)
- **Proper nouns:** none
- **Exercises:** `okoa` rescue, `pelesiden` president, `mungi`-before-numeral
  approximation, agentless predication (intransitive + impersonal), a date
  (`dinka ta` = Sunday), reported speech by juxtaposition.

## English

A big flood struck the city on Sunday. Many houses broke. A team rescued about
twenty people. The president said: the government helps all people.

## Talo

```talo
# 1 — date is a noun phrase: dinka ta = "day one" = Sunday (0008 §4)
sailabaka gandepe tatakuto kotaka dinka ta   › A big flood struck the city on Sunday.
# gloss: flood-N big-MOD strike-V city-N day-N one

# 2 — agentless as intransitive change-of-state (0008 §1)
baitika ingi pecato li                        › Many houses broke.
# gloss: house-N many break-V COMPLETIVE

# 3 — okoa "rescue"; mungi precedes the numeral it hedges (0008 §3); 20 = nu diko
kelopoka okoato hitoka mungi nu diko          › A team rescued about twenty people.
# gloss: group-N rescue-V person-N about two ten

# 4–5 — reported speech as juxtaposition (0008 §2); ote "all" determiner
pelesidenka semato li                         › The president said:
# gloss: president-N say-V COMPLETIVE
seifuka tolonato hitoka ote                    › the government helps all people.
# gloss: government-N help-V person-N all
```
