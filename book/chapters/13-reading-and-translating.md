# Chapter 13 — Reading and translating

You now know the whole language. This last chapter is practice: a short reading
that uses what you have learned, and exercises that ask you to translate **both
ways** — Talo into English, and English into Talo.

## Reading: a new school

```talo
# Read it through once before looking at the translation below.
Belayabaitika nayape bukato li kotaka lo.            › A new school opened in the city.
Hitoka ingi datanto asagohanka lo.                   › Many people came in the morning.
Ayalikika semato ce totoka pu bekito bacato i tulito. › The teacher said that the children should read and write.
Totoka somape tauto lebi.                            › Children who study know more.
Inino kotaka kunato belayabaitika haope.             › Now the city has a good school.
```

Look at how much is doing work here: a compound (`belayabaitika` "learn-house" =
school), a built word (`ayalikika` "teacher"), a reported clause with `ce`, a
helping verb (`bekito` "should"), and a describing verb (`somape` "who study").
None of it is special — it is all the same handful of rules.

## How to translate into Talo

Work in this order, every time:

1. **Find the subject** and put it first.
2. **Badge every content word** — `-ka` thing, `-to` action, `-pe` description.
3. **Put the small words where they belong** — role markers and aspect *after*
   their word; determiners *after* the noun; `ne` and helping verbs *before* the
   verb.
4. **Read it back** against the rules. If each badge is right and the subject is
   first, it is correct.

## Exercises

**13.1 (Talo → English)** Translate:

```talo
Mi tauto ce yu yato senanpe.
Kanuka hasilupe yato gandepe lebi nekoka fe.
```

**13.2 (English → Talo)** Translate each sentence:

- "The teacher should help the children."
- "I know that there is water in the house."
- "People who work are tired." (work `kasi`, tired `lela`)

**13.3 (English → Talo, short prose)** Translate this little paragraph:

> A small dog came to the house. It wanted food. The child gave it water. Now the
> dog is happy.

**13.4** Re-read the school reading above and answer in English: what did the
teacher say the children should do?
