# Chapter 5 — Questions and saying "no"

## Dialogue

```talo
A:  Yu mauto cakulaka ke?    › Do you want food?
B:  Hi, mi mauto.            › Yes, I do (want).
A:  Yu mauto seko?           › What do you want?
B:  Mi mauto panika.         › I want water.
A:  Mi ne mauto cakulaka.    › I don't want food.
```

## Grammar: yes/no questions with ke

To turn any statement into a yes/no question, add `ke` at the **end**. Nothing
else changes — no word-order flip, no extra verb.

```talo
Kanuka tumito.       › The dog runs.
Kanuka tumito ke?    › Does the dog run?
Yu yato edukika ke?  › Are you a teacher?
```

`ke` must be the **last** word:

```talo
* kanuka ke tumito     › ★ wrong: ke must come at the very end
Kanuka tumito ke.      › Does the dog run?
```

To answer, use `hi` (yes) or `no` (no).

## Grammar: content questions stay in place

For "what / who / where / when / why / how" questions, Talo uses a question word —
and leaves it exactly where the answer would go. Nothing moves to the front.

| Talo | English |
|---|---|
| `sela` | who |
| `seko` | what |
| `selo` | where |
| `seno` | when |
| `sefu` | why |
| `sewa` | how |

```talo
Yu mauto seko?       › You want what?  (= What do you want?)
Sela tumito?         › Who runs?
Te ikuto selo?       › Where is he going?
```

Compare with the answer — the question word simply sits where the answer sits:

```talo
Mi mauto panika.     › I want water.
Mi mauto seko?       › I want what?
```

No fronting, no "do"-support, no movement. This is one of the easiest question
systems of any language.

## Grammar: negation with ne

To say "not", put `ne` directly **before** the word you are negating — usually the
verb:

```talo
Kanuka ne tumito.    › The dog does not run.
Mi ne mauto.         › I don't want (it).
```

You can also negate something other than the verb by placing `ne` before it:

```talo
Ne kanuka tumito.    › Not the DOG runs (something else does).
```

## Vocabulary

| Talo | English |
|---|---|
| mau | want |
| ke | (yes/no question marker) |
| hi / no | yes / no |
| ne | not |
| sela seko selo seno sefu sewa | who what where when why how |

**5.3** Make negative: "The cat does not eat." / "I don't run."
