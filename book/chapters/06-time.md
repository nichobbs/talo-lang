# Chapter 6 — Time, completion, and "more than one"

## Dialogue

```talo
A:  Yu manuto li ke?         › Have you eaten?
B:  Hi, mi manuto li.        › Yes, I have eaten.
A:  Mi manuto wi.            › I am eating (right now).
B:  Keso mi manuto cakulaka. › Tomorrow I (will) eat food.
```

## Grammar: Talo has no tenses

This is good news: there is nothing to conjugate. A plain Talo verb is timeless —
`manuto` can mean "eat", "eats", "ate", or "will eat" depending on context. When
the time matters, you simply **say when** with a time-word:

| Talo | English |
|---|---|
| `leo` | today |
| `keso` | tomorrow |
| `yana` | yesterday |
| `inino` | now |

```talo
Mi tumito yana.      › I ran yesterday.
Mi tumito keso.      › I will run tomorrow.
```

The verb never changes. You just add the when-word.

## Grammar: two optional aspect markers

If you want to stress whether an action is **finished** or **ongoing**, add a small
word right after the verb:

| marker | meaning |
|---|---|
| `li` | completed — done, has happened |
| `wi` | ongoing — is happening, -ing |

```talo
Kanuka tumito.       › The dog runs / ran.  (unmarked)
Kanuka tumito li.    › The dog has run.       (completed)
Kanuka tumito wi.    › The dog is running.    (ongoing)
```

These come **immediately after** the verb, and only after a verb:

```talo
* kanuka wi tumito     › ★ wrong: the aspect word goes after the verb
Kanuka tumito wi.      › The dog is running.
```

## Grammar: plural with pu

A plain noun in Talo is neither singular nor plural — `kanuka` can be "a dog" or
"dogs". When you specifically mean more than one, add `pu` after the noun:

```talo
Kanuka pu tumito.    › The dogs run.
```

This is also how the pronouns become plural: `mi pu` "we", `yu pu` "you all",
`te pu` "they".

```talo
Mi pu manuto cakulaka.   › We eat food.
```

For an exact count, use a number word instead (numbers come after the noun, like
other determiners):

```talo
Mi motuto kanuka nu.     › I have two dogs.
```

(`nu` is "two"; `motu` is "have".)

## Vocabulary

| Talo | English |
|---|---|
| li / wi | completed / ongoing |
| pu | (plural) |
| leo keso yana inino | today tomorrow yesterday now |
| motu | have |
| ta nu mo | one two three |
