# Answer key

**Ch. 1** — **1.1** pani 2, baiti 2, makan 2, gou 1, kepala 3. **1.2** `blu` and
`pri` are impossible (a word may not begin with two consonants); `tak` is
impossible (it ends in `k`; only `n` may close a word). `kanu` and `sun` are fine.
**1.3** Stress on the first syllable: **WA**-ni-ta, **CA**-ku-la, **NE**-ko.

**Ch. 2** — **2.1** `panika`, `nekoka`, `manuka`. **2.2** `tumito`, `manuto`.
**2.3** `Nekoka manuto.` / `Pikope kanuka tumito.` **2.4** `neko` needs a badge:
`Nekoka manuto.`

**Ch. 3** — **3.1** `Mi yato senanpe.` / `Yu yato edukika.` / `Nekoka yato
pikope.` **3.2** `gandepe kanuka` is a *thing* ("a big dog"); `kanuka yato gandepe`
is a *statement* ("the dog is big"). **3.3** `Satoka yato dobuka.`

**Ch. 4** — **4.1** `Mi donato cakulaka nekoka na.` **4.2** `Hitoka tumito baitika
su.` **4.3** e.g. `Hitoka kanuka kanto.` and `Hitoka kanto kanuka.` **4.4** The
subject must come first; `donato` (the verb) cannot start the sentence.

**Ch. 5** — **5.1** `Kanuka manuto ke?` / `Yu yato senanpe ke?` **5.2** `Yu kanto
seko?` / `Sela manuto?` **5.3** `Nekoka ne manuto.` / `Mi ne tumito.` **5.4**
`Yu mauto seko?` — the question word stays in place; do not move it to the front.

**Ch. 6** — **6.1** `Mi manuto li.` / `Mi manuto wi.` **6.2** `Kanuka tumito
yana.` **6.3** `Nekoka pu tumito.` **6.4** `Mi manuto li.` — the aspect word goes
*after* the verb.

**Ch. 7** — **7.1** `pikakika` a cook, `pikadeka` a kitchen. **7.2** `gandekupe`.
**7.3** `kanucika` puppy (little dog), `kanugoka` big hound. **7.4** `gandetato`.

**Ch. 8** — **8.1** `olo`, `bako`, `hala`, `seno`. **8.2** `Bala tumito.` / `Mi
tauto hako.` / `Ola kunato inilo.` **8.3** `bawa` = some + way = "somehow"; `ono`
= every + time = "always".

**Ch. 9** — **9.1** `Fi yu manuto, mi yato senanpe.` **9.2** `Kanuka kunato baitika
lo.` **9.3** `Mi kunato inilo.` / `Panika kunato ke?` **9.4** `yato` for "the cat
is small"; `kunato` for "the cat is in the house".

**Ch. 10** — **10.1** e.g. `Ahoi! Mi yato Milaka. Tanku.` **10.2** `Selo yato
panika?` / `Itu kunato baitika lo.` **10.3** (your own — check each content word
ends in `-ka`, `-to`, or `-pe`.)

**Ch. 11** — **11.1** `Te kanto sendi.` / `Ayalikika sendi datanto li.` **11.2**
`Te tolonato salin.` **11.3** `baitika nupe`, `dinka tape`. **11.4** `Mi datanto
nu kai.` / `ta bagi mo`.

**Ch. 12** — **12.1** `Kanuka yato gandepe lebi nekoka fe.` **12.2** `hitoka
tolonape`, `nekoka makanpe`. **12.3** `Yu bekito datanto.` / `Mi ne bisato
makanto.` **12.4** `Mi tauto ce yu yato senanpe.`

**Ch. 13** — **13.1** "I know that you are happy." / "The running dog is bigger
than the cat." **13.2** `Ayalikika bekito tolonato totoka pu.` / `Mi tauto ce
panika kunato baitika lo.` / `Hitoka kasipe yato lelape.` **13.3** e.g. `Kanuka
pikope datanto baitika su. Te mauto cakulaka. Totoka donato panika te na. Inino
kanuka yato senanpe.` **13.4** They should read and write (`bacato i tulito`).
