# Teach Yourself Talo

*A constructed auxiliary language built for the first-ever second-language learner.*

Talo is a small, regular, no-exceptions language. It was designed so that someone
learning their **first** foreign language could pick it up by recognising words
they half-know already and by following a handful of rules that never bend. There
are no verb conjugations to memorise, no genders, no irregular plurals, no silent
letters, and no spelling you cannot predict from the sound.

This book teaches Talo from nothing. Each chapter has four parts:

- a short **dialogue** or reading in Talo, with a translation;
- a **grammar** section that explains exactly what is new, and why;
- a **vocabulary** list of the words introduced;
- **exercises** (answers are in the back).

You do not need any background in linguistics. Where a grammar term appears, it is
explained in plain words the first time.

> **How Talo is written.** Every letter is always pronounced the same way, and
> every word is stressed on its first syllable. Once you know the twenty letters
> (Chapter 1), you can read any Talo word aloud correctly on sight — there are no
> exceptions to learn later.

A note on the examples: every Talo sentence in this book has been checked by the
Talo grammar validator, so nothing here is accidentally ungrammatical. Sentences
marked with a small star (★) are *deliberately wrong* — they show a common mistake
so you can learn to avoid it.

Let us begin.
