# Chapter 10 — Everyday Talo: a short phrasebook

You now know the whole grammar. This chapter is practical: greetings, politeness,
and a worked everyday conversation you can adapt.

## Greetings and politeness

These are fixed little words (no badge):

| Talo | English |
|---|---|
| `ahoi` | hello / hi |
| `cao` | goodbye |
| `doso` | please |
| `tanku` | thank you |
| `pole` | sorry |
| `oke` | okay |
| `kalibu` | welcome |

```talo
A:  Ahoi! Yu yato sela?       › Hello! Who are you?
B:  Mi yato Milaka. Tanku.    › I am Mila. Thank you.
A:  Doso, yu tolonato mi.     › Please, help me.
B:  Oke. Cao!                 › Okay. Goodbye!
```

(`tolona` help.)

## A meal

```talo
A:  Yu mauto cakulaka ke?         › Do you want food?
B:  Hi, doso. Mi mauto panika ti. › Yes, please. I want water too.
A:  Kunato cakulaka baitika lo.   › There is food in the house.
B:  Tanku sana!                   › Thank you very much!
```

(`ti` also/too, `sana` very.)

## Getting around

```talo
A:  Selo yato baitika?        › Where is the house?
B:  Itu kunato itulo.         › It is over there.
A:  Mi ikuto itulo.           › I go there.
```

## A tiny story

```talo
# Read this slowly; it uses only what you have learned.
Kanuka kunato baitika lo.     › A dog is in the house.
Te mauto panika.              › It wants water.
Hitoka donato panika te na.   › The person gives water to it.
Inino kanuka yato senanpe.    › Now the dog is happy.
```
